class = "ex1">TITLE 1476. GAO study on the effectiveness and impact of various appraisal methods, valuation models and distributions channels, and on the Home Valuation Code of conduct and the Appraisal Subcommittee.
class = "ex5">(a) In general. The Government Accountability Office shall conduct a study on
class = "ex6">(1) the effectiveness and impact of
class = "ex7">(A) appraisal methods, including the cost approach, the comparative sales approach, the income approach, and others that may be available;
class = "ex7">(B) appraisal valuation models, including licensed and certified appraisals, broker-priced opinions, and automated valuation models; and
class = "ex7">(C) appraisal distribution channels, including appraisal management companies, independent appraisal operations within mortgage originators, and fee-for-service appraisers;
class = "ex6">(2) the Home Valuation Code of Conduct; and
class = "ex6">(3) the Appraisal Subcommittees functions pursuant to title XI of the Financial Institutions Reform, Recovery, and Enforcement Act of 1989.
class = "ex5">(b) Study. Not later than
class = "ex6">(1) 12 months after the date of enactment of this Act, the Government Accountability Office shall submit a study to the Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senate and the Committee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives; and
class = "ex6">(2) 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the Government Accountability Office shall provide a report on the status of the study and any preliminary findings to the Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senate and the Committee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives.
class = "ex5">(c) Content of study. The study required by this section shall include an examination of the following:
class = "ex6">(1) Appraisal approaches, valuation models, and distribution channels.
class = "ex7">(A) The prevalence, alone or in combination, of certain appraisal approaches, models, and channels in purchase-money and refinance mortgage transactions.
class = "ex7">(B) The accuracy of these approaches, models, and channels in assessing the property as collateral.
class = "ex7">(C) Whether and how these approaches, models, and channels contributed to price speculation during the previous cycle.
class = "ex7">(D) The costs to consumers of these approaches, models, and channels.
class = "ex7">(E) The disclosure of fees to consumers in the appraisal process.
class = "ex7">(F) To what extent the usage of these approaches, models, and channels may be influenced by a conflict of interest between the mortgage lender and the appraiser and the mechanism by which the lender selects and compensates the appraiser.
class = "ex7">(G) The suitability of these approaches, models, and channels in rural versus urban areas.
class = "ex6">(2) Home valuation code of conduct (HVCC).
class = "ex7">(A) How the HVCC affects mortgage lenders selection of appraisers.
class = "ex7">(B) How the HVCC affects State regulation of appraisers and appraisal distribution channels.
class = "ex7">(C) How the HVCC affects the quality and cost of appraisals and the length of time to obtain an appraisal.
class = "ex7">(D) How the HVCC affects mortgage brokers, small businesses, and consumers.
class = "ex5">(d) Additional study required.
class = "ex6">(1) In general. Not later than 18 months after the date of enactment of this Act, the Government Accountability Office shall submit a study to the Committee on Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs of the Senate and the Committee on Financial Services of the House of Representatives.
class = "ex6">(2) Content of additional study. The study required under paragraph (1) shall include
class = "ex7">(A) an examination of
class = "ex8">(i) the Appraisal Subcommittees ability to monitor and enforce State and Federal certification requirements and standards, including by providing a summary with a statistical breakdown of enforcement actions taken during the last 10 years;
class = "ex8">(ii) whether existing Federal financial institutions regulatory agency exemptions on appraisals for federally related transactions needs to be revised; and
class = "ex8">(iii) whether new means of data collection, such as the establishment of a national repository, would benefit the Appraisal Subcommittees ability to perform its functions; and
class = "ex7">(B) recommendations from this examination for administrative and legislative action at the Federal and State level.

Purpose

Classify the logical parts of a sentence.

  • Antecedent L1: antecedent parts describe cases in which the law can be applied. L1 stands for first layer.
  • Antecedent L2: are antecendent parts that further describe an antecendent part. L2 stands for second layer.
  • Antecedent L3: are antecendent parts of layer 3.
  • Consequent: consequent parts describe the provision of the law.
  • Topic A: topic type A is a subject related to the antecedent part.
  • Topic C: topic type C is a subject related to the consequent part.
  • Topic A+C: topic type A+C is a subject related to both, the antecedent and the consequent part.
  • Left Equivalent: left equivalent is the left part of a sentence with two equivalent statements.
  • Right Equivalent: right equivalent is the right part of a sentence with two equivalent statements.
  • Headlines: are already preclassified and function as a guideline for the user.

How it works

Classification:

  • Select a part of a sentence.
  • Click on the appropriate classification on the right-hand side.
  • The part is colored accordingly.

Layers:

  • Always first classify the first layer before classifying the second layer.
  • Highlight the part of L1 that is to be classified as L2.
  • Click on the appropriate classification on the right-hand side.

Corrections:

  • To delete the classification before you saved, right-click on the corresponding part of the sentene in the text.
  • To delete the classification after saving, highlight the section and click on "Remove" on the bottom-right.
  • Now, you may classify it again.

Save:

  • Click on the SAVE ! button on the bottom right.
  • Attention!You have to manually save your results before switching titles or logging out. Otherwise your classifications will be deleted. Recommended is saving after a sentence has been classified successfully.

Further instructions:

  • Always include parenthesis into the part of sentence.
  • Always include the punctuation characters at the end of a part of sentence.

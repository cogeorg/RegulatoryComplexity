Purpose

Classify the logical parts of a sentence.

  • Antecedent L1: antecedent parts describe cases in which the law can be applied. L1 stands for first layer.
  • Antecedent L2: are antecendent parts that further describe an antecendent part. L2 stands for second layer.
  • Antecedent L3: are antecendent parts of layer 3.
  • Consequent: consequent parts describe the provision of the law.
  • Topic A: topic type A is a subject related to the antecedent part.
  • Topic C: topic type C is a subject related to the consequent part.
  • Topic A+C: topic type A+C is a subject related to both, the antecedent and the consequent part.
  • Left Equivalent: left equivalent is the left part of a sentence with two equivalent statements.
  • Right Equivalent: right equivalent is the right part of a sentence with two equivalent statements.
  • Headlines: are already preclassified and function as a guideline for the user.

Examples

Example 1

The term ``bank holding company'' has the same meaning as in section 2 of the Bank Holding Company Act of 1956 (12 U.S.C. 1841).

Example 2

A foreign bank or company that is treated as a bank holding company for purposes of the Bank Holding Company Act of 1956, pursuant to section 8(a) of the International Banking Act of 1978 (12 U.S.C. 3106(a)), shall be treated as a bank holding company for purposes of this title.

Example 3

The term ``foreign nonbank financial company'' means a company (other than a company that is, or is treated in the United States as, a bank holding company) that is (i) incorporated or organized in a country other than the United States; and (ii) predominantly engaged in, including through a branch in the United States, financial activities, as defined in paragraph (6).

Example 4

The Commission may require any investment adviser registered under this title ``(A) to maintain such records of, and file with the Commission such reports regarding, private funds advised by the investment adviser, as necessary and appropriate in the public interest and for the protection of investors, or for the assessment of systemic risk by the Financial Stability Oversight Council (in this subsection referred to as the `Council'); and ``(B) to provide or make available to the Council those reports or records or the information contained therein.

How it works

Classification:

  • Select a part of a sentence.
  • Click on the appropriate classification on the right-hand side.
  • The part is colored accordingly.

Layers:

  • Always first classify the first layer before classifying the second layer.
  • Highlight the part of L1 that is to be classified as L2.
  • Click on the appropriate classification on the right-hand side.

Corrections:

  • To delete the classification, right-click on the corresponding part of the sentene in the text.
  • Now, you may classify it again.
  • Attention! When deleting a layered part, right-click on the L2 part.

Save:

  • Click on the SAVE ! button on the bottom right.
  • Careful! Once you have saved your results, you cannot make corrections anymore.
  • You have to manually save your results before switching titles or logging out. Otherwise your classifications will be deleted. Recommended is saving after a sentence has been classified successfully.

Further instructions:

  • Always include parenthesis into the part of sentence.
  • Always include the punctuation characters at the end of a part of sentence.
  • Always leave a whitespace between parts of sentence.
